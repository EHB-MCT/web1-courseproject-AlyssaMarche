Who am I?

I’m Harry Potter, and my journey began at Hogwarts, where I spent six unforgettable years. Sorted into Gryffindor, I dove into the world of magic, learning spells, brewing potions, and flying on broomsticks. I even joined the Quidditch team as a Seeker, where my job was to catch the elusive Golden Snitch. And, more often than not, I led my team to victory. But my life wasn’t just about school. I didn’t even finish my seventh year—because I was too busy hunting Horcruxes. These dark, magical objects were tied to the soul of my greatest enemy, Voldemort. He had killed my parents in an attempt to destroy me, but I survived. Ever since, he’s tried to end me, driven by the belief that I was the one who would bring about his downfall. His followers, the Death Eaters, made my life miserable.
But I kept fighting. By my sixth year, I was already facing death head-on. Yet, the battle wasn’t over. It was my final confrontation with him that led to the end of his reign of terror. After everything, I even ended up working for the Ministry of Magic—despite their disbelief when I tried to warn them about Voldemort’s return during my fifth year. It’s funny how people who once doubted me would later rely on me. But I proved myself. Not just as the "Boy Who Lived," but as someone who helped shape the future of the wizarding world.
